Data Preservation Initiatives

Some Data Preservation Initiatives include the Rosetta Project, which is a global collaboration of language specialists and native speakers working to build a publically accesible digital library of human languages.

And the Internet Archive, a nonprofit digital library that provided free access to collections of digitalized materials, ranging from websites, software applications, music, audiovisual and print materials, which is no longer available.

Digital Archive Journalism In Relation To The Digital Dark Age

The existence of "Digital Archive Journalism" is a form of Data Preservation Initiatives as a publically accessible digital library/archive.

The interlinking of Digital Archive Journals makes source material and information easier to find, connecting to digital sources and other journals.

Maintaining the Digital Archive Journals with the same formats as the preservation of Lost Media allows for the prevention of the loss of data.

It also spreads the importance of preservation of information to the general public regarding digital preservation of data and information, along with digital preservation of journalism for certain topics that are not easily accessible outside of research discourses.

Conclusions

Digital Archive Journalism is a step towards preservation of information that is not easily accessible to the general public, and putting it in a format that grants them easy access to the information.

Digital Archive Journalism is also a step towards a form of preservation to attempt to overcome the Digital Dark Age.
