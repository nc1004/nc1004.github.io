Music

Music has always been a part of our lives in one way or another, from lullabies to background music of stores. For some, it's a matter of casual listening, to enjoy a few songs and go about your day, for others it may be a little more; to pay attention to the artists, to focus on lyrics, to enjoy the experience of concerts, and so on. Music is deeply intertwined with the context of society, in culture, economics, and politics. Some genres of music are created to go against norms of the context, to deliver a message, or to bring people together. In the case of the Massachusetts Rock Against Racism, it was all three.

Massachusetts Rock Against Racism

The Massachusetts Rock Against Racism (RAR) was co-founded in the Boston area in 1979, the RAR movement beginning in mid-1970s England, when Boston and its surrounding areas were teeming with racism. The organization was founded by a group of volunteers, ranging from musicians, radio announcers, educators, and students. It used music and mass media, ranging from presentations, concerts, festivals, broadcast and TV productions, workshops, performances, community screenings, and print and media coverage, to confront racism through students and the community. (Healey Library, Digital Collections).

Digital Archive Journal

A "Digital Archive Journal" is a record of news and events dealing with a particular subject in digital format with source material originating from an Archive. This particular journal is my perspective of the events regarding the Massachusetts Rock Against Racism organization's youth and their message.

Source material of this journal originates from the University of Massachusetts Boston: Massachusetts Rock Against Racism archival collections (both digital and physical), peer-reviewed sources from Ronald P. Formisano and Ryan Moore, other journal sources from Mass Cult 617 and Boston Institute For Non-Profit Journalism, and additional online sources from Reddit and Genius.
