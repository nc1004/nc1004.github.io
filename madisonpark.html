Spreading the Message

Racism in the 1970s was deeply rooted in class conflicts and white backlash, affecting schools from the scholarship level to neighborhood suburbs.

The youth who were affected, along with the organization made decisions to spread the message of RAR through various means. Some of the most notable means of spreading their message were through brochures and projects such as festivals and mass media.

What the Message Entailed

Mass. RAR continued to spread the message through the culmination of youth-led activities, the message they sent entailed spreading awareness of youth culture through music and educating against racism.

How the Message was so widespread

Efforts of the youth in Mass. RAR to educate the public against racism through music by means of performances and mass media spread greatly. This message was so widespread due to the organization's efforts to make it easily accessible to the public.
